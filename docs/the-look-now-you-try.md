# "The Look" — Phase 1 "Now you try" copy (the second object)

The conversion moment at the end of the bowl experience (see `the-look-bowl-copy.md`, Stage Final). The visitor looks at a new object with no guidance, then asks for the reveals. The reveals are deliberately written to echo the five moves they just learned on the bowl — that echo is the point. It tells them the skill transfers, which is what makes a sleuth.

## The object

- **Pitcher**, black-on-white, US Southwest. Met, public domain.
- Source for ingestion: grammar-collections MCP → `met` / `met-833901`.
- Image URL (long edge 1568): `https://images.metmuseum.org/CRDImages/ao/original/DP-24281-002.jpg`

- Chosen on purpose: a different shape than the bowl — neck, handle, a chipped rim — so the visitor transfers the skill instead of repeating it. No sensitivity flag (the system withheld the culturally sensitive Mimbres bowls; this is clean).

## The free look

> Your turn. Here's another pot — a pitcher this time, with a neck and a handle. Nobody's going to point. Look at it for a while. Find one thing. How it was made, how it moves, where it got hurt — anything.

Button: **Show me what I might have missed.**

---

## The reveals (light — each one echoes the bowl)

Keep these short. The visitor already has the toolkit; this is a victory lap, not a lesson. Each reveal names the move and tells them they already found its twin on the bowl.

**Movement — the spirals**
Highlight: the two big spirals on the body.
> See the two big spirals? They wind your eye inward and set the whole pot turning — same trick the bowl pulled, a different way. You already know how to spot this.

**It got hurt — the rim**
Highlight: the chipped, broken edge of the rim.
> Look at the top edge. Pieces are missing — the rim's been chipped and broken. Like the bowl, this thing has been through something and survived it.

**The hand — the line-fill**
Highlight: the fine parallel lines filling the dark shapes.
> Look inside the dark shapes — they're filled with thin lines, brushed by hand, one after another. Get close and the spacing wanders. A person did this, freehand, the same way they painted the bowl.

**One color, figure and ground — the sawtooth**
Highlight: the white zigzag running between the dark bands.
> One paint again, on bare clay. And look at the white zigzag between the dark shapes — the empty space is a shape too. You caught that on the bowl. Here it is again.

**The honest one — the meaning**
Highlight: pull back to the whole pot.
> So what do the spirals mean? Same honest answer as before: we don't know. People love to say a spiral is water, or wind, or a journey. Maybe. But the potter didn't leave a note, and a good guess isn't a fact. You knowing the difference — that's the part that matters.
